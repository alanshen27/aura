# Safety

## Overview

Safety is a core consideration in every step of our project. We are committed to following iGEM safety guidelines and ensuring our work poses minimal risk to people, animals, and the environment.

## Lab Safety

- All team members have completed biosafety training before entering the lab
- We work exclusively with Risk Group 1 organisms
- Standard PPE (lab coats, gloves, safety goggles) is worn at all times
- All biological waste is autoclaved before disposal
- We follow our institution's biosafety protocols and have approval from the Institutional Biosafety Committee (IBC)

## Project-Specific Safety

### Organism Selection

We chose to work with non-pathogenic chassis organisms that are well-characterized and commonly used in synthetic biology research. Our organisms do not pose a threat to human health or the environment.

### Containment

Our biosensor is designed for in-vitro use only — it would be applied to milk samples in a contained diagnostic device, not released into the environment or used inside living animals.

### Dual-Use Considerations

We have assessed our project for dual-use potential. Our biosensor technology is specific to mastitis detection and does not have obvious misuse potential. The biological components we use are standard and widely available.

## Environmental Safety

- No genetically modified organisms will be released into the environment
- Our detection system is cell-free / contained, reducing environmental risk
- All experiments are conducted within BSL-1 facilities
