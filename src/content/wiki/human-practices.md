# Integrated Human Practices

## Overview

Our human practices work explores the real-world context of mastitis detection — talking to dairy farmers, veterinarians, food safety experts, and other stakeholders to ensure our project addresses actual needs.

## Stakeholder Engagement

### Dairy Farmers

We conducted interviews with local dairy farmers to understand:

- How they currently detect mastitis in their herds
- The financial impact of mastitis on their operations
- What an ideal detection tool would look like for them
- Barriers to adopting new technologies on the farm

*Key insight: Farmers need something fast, cheap, and usable without specialized training.*

### Veterinarians

We consulted with veterinary professionals to understand:

- Current diagnostic gold standards and their limitations
- How early detection could change treatment outcomes
- What biological markers are most reliable for detection

### Food Safety Regulators

We reached out to food safety authorities to learn about:

- Milk quality testing requirements
- Regulations around biosensor-based diagnostics
- Pathways to get novel detection tools approved

---

## How This Shaped Our Project

Our stakeholder conversations directly influenced our project design:

1. **Simplicity** — we prioritized a detection system that requires minimal training
2. **Cost** — we aim for a per-test cost well below current PCR methods
3. **Speed** — our target is results within minutes, not days
4. **Accessibility** — designed for on-farm use, not just lab settings
